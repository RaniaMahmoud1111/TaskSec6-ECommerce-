What is a Unix Timestamp?

A Unix timestamp (also called Epoch time) represents time as the number of seconds (or milliseconds) since January 1, 1970, 00:00:00 UTC.

Types of Unix Timestamps

  • Seconds-based (10-digit): Example: 1711733567
  • Milliseconds-based (13-digit): Example: 1711733567123
